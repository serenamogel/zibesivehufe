Who Is Rickie Fowler’s Sister Taylor Fowler?

Born in Murrieta, California, Rickie Fowler’s sister, Taylor Fowler, played softball until her final year of high school at Murrieta Valley High, beginning at 6.

She was forced to join the golf team by a buddy during her junior year of high school and decided to try it out because the squad needed players.

Once Fowler started participating, she discovered how much she liked the sport and kept playing until her senior year. She had a prior athletic background, so golf came relatively quickly to her.

Fowler received assistance from an instructor for an error at a nearby shooting range. She knew she still wanted to compete until she got to Cal State Fullerton after graduating high school.

Her Skills Earned Her Praise From Coaches

Neil Walton, a Titan assistant coach who coaches the women’s golf team, started at shortstop for the Titans’ 2004 national championship squad.

He returned to school to help athletes with their mental games and earned a bachelor’s degree in sports psychology. In Walton’s opinion, Fowler had the tenacity to pursue her goals to the fullest extent.

He said that Taylor hasn’t been competing as long as some other players, but she has an edge since she’s athletic. She works hard and can perform at an extremely high level.

She Called Her Brother A Motivator

Fowler’s brother, Rickie, made his PGA Tour debut in September 2009 and was named PGA Rookie of the Year in 2010.

After seeing his lifestyle, she gets a glimpse of what being a professional may be like. She said that it is undoubtedly a motivator, but she wasn’t sure if she would continue playing golf when she graduates and waited till she was a senior before making a decision.

Moreover, Fowler had the fourth-best scoring average on the team during the 2009–10 campaign. Her successes from the 2013 autumn included being in the top five in an invitational and going under par in another competition.

Her major is business, but she hasn’t decided on a focus. According to Daily Titans, she hoped that the golf industry’s business model would benefit from her business studies in the future.

Taylor Fowler’s Golf Career With The Titans

For the Titans’ resurgent women’s golf program in 2009–10, Taylor participated in 19 rounds spread over seven competitions, with an 81.6 team scoring average (1,550 strokes).

Her best performance came in a tie for ninth place at the Cal State Bakersfield Spring Invitational on March 22–23, and her best round came on March 1 at the Fresno State Lexus Classic with a score of 70.

At the 2010 Big West Conference Championships, she shot a 34-over par 250, good for 28th place overall and the second-best score by a Titan.

She Was The Titans Top Scorer In 2010-11

She finished third in the dual match against Cal State Bakersfield with a 76 and tied for ninth at the Bobcat Invitational (11 over par 153) in Bozeman, Montana, and the Colby Invitational (12 over par 156) in San Jose.

Similarly, she shot a 74 in both the first round of the Folino Invitational in March and the first competition of the year, the Bobcat Invitational, both in September.

In August 2011, at the Hooters Tourney in Georgia, she and Deidre Crabtree collaborated to tie for 17th place out of 55 teams.

Rickie And Taylor’s Parents: Rod And Lynn

Fowler’s parents, Lynn and Rod Fowler, have monitored their children’s success since they installed a temporary golf course in their backyard.

Rod, Rickie’s dad, used to ride dirt bikes and now operates a trucking business. Before Rickie gave up bike racing to concentrate on golf after an injury in high school, Rod and his son used to ride motorcycles together.

According to the Golf Channel, his mother, Lynn, raced in a five-woman team and finished first in a 24-hour mountain biking race in Idylwild, California. Rickie acknowledged his mother’s driving prowess as she hurried back from the airport to see the conclusion of his event.

Rickie’s mother is Native American (Navajo) and Japanese, according to Golf.com. When World War II broke out, Lynn’s father was hauled from their Pomona, California home and placed in a Japanese internment camp in Heart Mountain, Wyoming.
