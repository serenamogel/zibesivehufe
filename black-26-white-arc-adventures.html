Black &amp; White arc (Adventures)

Publish date: 2024-03-20

From Bulbapedia, the community-driven Pokémon encyclopedia.

Black & White

Several characters

The Black & White arc (Japanese: ブラック・ホワイト編 Black & White arc), also referred to as The Tenth Chapter (Japanese: 第十章 The Tenth Chapter), is the tenth story arc of the Pokémon Adventures manga, consisting of nine volumes. It began serialization in September 2010, and ended in June 2013.

This arc follows the adventures of Black, who is on a quest to become the Champion of the Pokémon League. He is joined by White, a young woman who owns the Pokémon acting company, the BW Agency, and aims to raise the best Pokémon actor. As they travel through the Unova region, they encounter the organization known as Team Plasma, which is aiming to separate Pokémon from their Trainers, and their mysterious king, N.

Volumes

Mini-volumes

Main article: List of Pokémon Black and White volumes

Before the collection of chapters from the Black & White arc of Pokémon Adventures began in Volume 43 in Japan, the chapters were collected directly from the magazine publications into mini-graphic novels by VIZ Media in North America. These volumes, published under the title Pokémon Black and White and advertised as "a Pokémon Adventures special edition", retail for $4.99 USD and contain about three or four chapters each. The first two volumes were released on July 5, 2011 with subsequent volumes following until the release of volume 20 which was released on February 3, 2015.

A box set of the first eight volumes was released on October 2, 2012 including a special full-color poster. A second box set, containing volumes 9 through 14, was released on September 2, 2014. The third and final box set, containing volumes 15 through 20 was released on September 1, 2015.

In other languages

French
Main article: List of Pokémon Black and White volumes (France)

A French translation of the Black & White arc has been produced separately in France by publisher Kurokawa. Unlike the VIZ Media versions, which are slimmer than a typical volume and only contain three or four chapters, the French versions are a more typical volume size and contain about eight chapters each. Like VIZ Media's version, these are collected directly from the magazine publication. The first of these volumes was published in September 2011 with subsequent releases until the release of Volume 9 on October 9, 2014.

German

A German translation of the Black & White arc has been produced in Germany by publisher Panini Comics. Unlike the VIZ Media versions, which are slimmer than a typical volume and only contain three or four chapters, the German versions are a more typical volume size and contain about eight chapters each. Like VIZ Media's version, these are collected directly from the magazine publication. The first of these volumes was published on October 8, 2013 with subsequent releases until the release of Volume 9 on August 17, 2015.
