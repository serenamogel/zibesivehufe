Early life and education

Jada Koren Pinkett, born in Baltimore, Maryland, received her name from her mother’s favorite soap opera actress, Jada Rowland.

Her mother, Adrienne Banfield-Norris, served as the head nurse at an inner-city clinic in Baltimore, while her father, Robsol Pinkett Jr., operated a construction company.

Although they married when Banfield-Norris became pregnant during high school, they later divorced after several months.

Jada was primarily raised by her mother and her grandmother, Marion Martin Banfield, a Jamaican-born social worker married to Dr. Gilbert Banfield, a family doctor.

Jada’s grandmother was a proactive individual who aimed to improve the community and enhance the world’s beauty.

Noticing her granddaughter’s passion for the performing arts, Banfield enrolled Jada in piano, tap dance, and ballet lessons.

Jada attended the Baltimore School for the Arts, where she formed a close friendship with her classmate, rapper Tupac Shakur, during a time when she was involved in drug dealing.

Her mother had previously battled heroin addiction. At the school, Jada focused on dance and theatre, graduating in 1989. Following her graduation, she spent a year at the North Carolina School of the Arts.

Personal Life

Jada Pinkett first encountered her future husband, superstar actor Will Smith, while visiting the set of his ’90s sitcom, “The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air.” She auditioned for the role of his character’s girlfriend, Lisa Wilkes, but it ultimately went to Nia Long, as Jada was deemed “too short” for the part.

Will and Jada started dating in 1995, shortly after he separated from Sheree Fletcher.

Will had harbored feelings for Jada even before their relationship began but had not acted on them.

They tied the knot in 1997 and have since had two children, Jaden Smith and Willow Smith, both of whom have established successful careers in the entertainment industry.

Additionally, Jada became the stepmother to Trey Smith, Will’s son from a previous marriage.

In July 2019, Jada openly acknowledged being in a romantic relationship with singer August Alsina during a period when she and Will were separated in 2016.

In 2018, Jada disclosed that she had been living with the autoimmune disease alopecia areata, which led to hair loss.

Despite undergoing numerous medical tests, she remained unable to pinpoint the exact cause of her alopecia.

Notably, Jada Pinkett Smith became a focal point of a memorable incident at the 2022 Oscars when Will Smith confronted comedian Chris Rock for making a joke about her baldness.
