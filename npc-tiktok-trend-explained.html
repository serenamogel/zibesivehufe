NPC TikTok Trend Explained: When Did This Start to Happen?

Publish date: 2024-10-02

NPC TikTok Trend Explained: Creators are always striving to remain ahead of the curve when it comes to establishing an online presence. NPC streaming is the new thing that everyone is doing on their For You page right now.

This trend is based on the fact that users do strange things over and over again. So what’s the deal? Here is a rundown of everything you need to

What is NPC Streaming?

NPC streaming is when the streamers do things that make them look alive, like how NPCs in video games often do the same things or say the same things over and over again.

In this case, TikTok streamers only interact, talk, or do something specific when a viewer gives them a gift. The type of interaction can rely on the type of gift.

Viewers can send different choices like emotes, for the streamer to answer.

For example, if a watcher sends an ice cream, the streamer might act like he or she is licking it and say, “Oh, ice cream, yum.”

When Did This Start to Happen?

Know Your Meme says that NPC streaming started to become more famous last year and that Japanese TikToker @natuecoco was the first person to do it.

She has worked with TikToker Satoyu0704. The two of them pretended to be NPCs and went on to see who could win the most gifts.

Over the past year, more people like Cherry Crush TV and PinkDollReal have started doing popular NPC live shows.

Why is This Popular?

People may be more interested in NPC streaming because they want to see the maker act like an AI or a video game character and want to see how long they can keep it up.

Also, compared to a normal stream, NPC streaming needs more interaction from the viewers because the streamer is constantly responding to what is said and sent to them.

Pop Crave says that music producer Timberland was the most popular viewer of 19-year-old Pinkydoll’s latest TikTok Live. Pinkydoll is a viral content creator who makes videos on TikTok.

Conclusion

NPC streaming is a trend where creators create live-action NPCs on their For You page. These streamers interact with viewers by giving them gifts, such as emotes, and responding to their messages. The popularity of NPC streaming began last year, with Japanese TikToker @natuecoco being the first to do so.

Over the past year, more popular NPC live shows have been created by celebrities like Cherry Crush TV and PinkDollReal. People are interested in seeing the creators act like AI or video game characters and the increased interaction required for NPC streaming.
